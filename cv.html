PhD Thesis

Anomaly detection aims to discover abnormal patterns hidden in multidimensional radar signals and images. This research field is essential in data mining for quickly isolating irregular or suspicious segments in large amounts of the database. Some examples are: Oil Slick, Turbulent ship wake, Levee anomaly, Archeology. Having many public and private data, but due to limited cost, SAR images are often unlabeled. With the raise of sel-supervised learning and deep neural networks, pretext features can be vectorized requiring little to no labeled data. The thesis investigation is primarily directed towards guiding the self-supervised learning process to extract representations suitable for anomaly detection. These caracteristics must cover SAR diversities aspect: Polarimetric and Interferometric channels, Multi-bands, Multi-looks representations and in particular, Phase informations of the complex-valued image signals.
